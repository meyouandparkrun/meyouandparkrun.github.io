Gawler, South Australia

Gawler is the oldest country town on the Australian mainland in the state of South Australia. It was named after the second Governor of the colony of South Australia, George Gawler. It is about 40â€“44 km north of the centre of the state capital, Adelaide, and is close to the major wine producing district of the Barossa Valley.

We had this one saved in our back pocket, as it's the closest Parkrun to Trent's parents. So when vintage started to kick off we called this one in. There was lots of running groups participating, but apart right at the start the path wasn't too crowded.
You start downhill and gain momentuem back as it levels out, you need to be careful not to try and keep the same pace otherwise you'll wear out. The way back is difficult, but the 5km mark is set back so you actually finish on a downhill.

Trent: 15th place @ 22:50 (14th male & 4th in age category)
Sara: 30th place @ 25:41 (6th female & rd in age category)
