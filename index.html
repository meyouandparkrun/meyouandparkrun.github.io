December 8th, 2018

Fingal Bay, New South Wales (AUS)

The course is run entirely on concrete paths. The course is mainly on the pathway that runs along Fingal Bay waterfront from Tomaree Rd to the boat ramp (south end). The start, second turn-around point and the finish will be in the park adjacent to the surf club. Participants commence at the surf club and turn left onto the path, heading all the way through to Tomaree Rd, turn back and head to the boat ramp for their first turn around at that end and then head back toward the start/finish area, where they will complete another turn around on the grassed area and head back toward the boat ramp. They will complete their final turn turnaround at the boat ramp then head back to the finish line at the surf club park.

December 1st, 2018

Stockton, New South Wales (AUS)

This is a flat 5km loop course that follows the Stockton foreshore walking track taking in views of the Hunter River, Newcastle Harbour, Nobbys Lighthouse and Stockton Beach. The course begins to the east of the iconic Stockton Ferry at the end of Mitchell St and curves across the grass and turns eastward towards the ocean. Following the foreshore walking track along Newcastle Harbour past the Lions Park and playground with views of Nobbys Headland and Lighthouse. Veering left at the split in the path takes you up the ‘hill’ where the detour rejoins the original path. The course loops back at the metal path divider before the carpark and Stockton Breakwall. Travelling back, veer left at the split which returns us towards the ferry terminal. Continue past the ferry terminal along the harbour towards the Stockton boat ramp and Sailing Club with its many resident pelicans. The track continues along the path to the turnaround point just past the Stockton Scout Hall. Return is via the boat ramp and ferry terminal to finish at the start/finish line.

November 17th, 2018

Shellharbour, New South Wales (AUS)

The is one of the most scenic courses you will find on the Global parkrun map. Overlooking one of the most popular surf beaches in the area, the parkrun will give the runners and their families and friends a wonderful experience each Saturday morning. The course is a one lap anti-clockwise that starts by the park’s kiosk and heads slightly north before going down a grass car track to the beach. Runners will then run along the beach for approximately 300m before heading back to a track that takes them around the eastern side of the lagoon. This also takes the runners up a 20m climb over 300m before it flattens out around the Northern end of the lagoon. The run then descends around to the Western side of the lagoon. The run then turns right and heads up a vehicle track to the Western side of the park near the park entrance. The vehicle track is approximately 1km long and climbs about 40m. Runners then follow the inside of the driveway fence remaining on the grass and head back to the finishline near the Kiosk.

October 27th, 2018

Warrnambool, Victoria (AUS)

Warrnambool parkrun is a 5km out and back course that begins and finishes in the Lake Pertobe Adventure Park, with the course running east alongside the lake before joining up with the Lake Pertobe Trail. The course crosses 2 bridges before turning right and running in an anticlockwise direction with the course then following the trail around the back of Lake Pertobe. The course continues down the back straight of Lake Pertobe, comes to a small rise before continuing down through 1 chicane and then on to the pathway alongside the Merri River. The course then U-turns and follows the same track back to the start/finish area at Lake Pertobe.
