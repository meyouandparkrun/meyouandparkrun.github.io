Forster, New South Wales

Forster is a coastal town in the Mid North Coast region of New South Wales, Australia, in the Mid-Coast Council LGA, about 308 km north-north-east of Sydney. It is immediately adjacent to its twin, Tuncurry, which is the smaller of the two towns. Forster is known for its stunning waters & Manning Valley beauty. At the 2016 census, the Forster-Tuncurry area had a population of 19,918 people, while the permanent population of Forster alone was 13,740.

After a week of false starts due to the ongoing bushfire crisis, it was finally safe for us to drive. The devaston was crazy and we still saw spot fires burning unattended.
We were at Forster during their X anniversary, and the local bowls club owner provided a variety of cakes for everyone. This made the finish line even tastier than usual. This course was quite calm as it was run on gravel trails and had good views of the oceanside. The path was a good size, with only a few non parkrunners. There is one main undulation before the turn around point, but coming back down right away gives you a breather.
